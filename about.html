About Vidjeti

Web 2.0 exploded about five years ago, but before then you could find My People haunting livejournal and getting pissed off at server outages. Put me and mine in a room and the sole guarantee is that in five minutes we'll be talking about what was on TV last night and - more importantly - why it matters. You want a take-down on the religious apocrypha in Battlestar Galactica? I know a girl who can help you with that.

My people: over-educated; underemployed; born-again believers in Netflix.

Nothing here is particularly high-brow, but I like to make the most of being in London, so there are a lot more posts on theatre now than there were five years ago, and you can find me abusing the re-tweet function at @vidjeti on twitter.

Deepings Raft Race

Every August the people of my hometown watch as a few brave souls race from one end of our barely-there river to the other, often dressed in costumes and nearly always soaked through. Rain or shine, the town floods the riverbank before wandering into the town centre to buy food.

If it sounds a bit mental, well, it is. At either end of the course someone sits and writes down the start and end times of each raft, and in the middle of the course everyone has to get out of the river to circumnavigate a lock. Even though DRR is held in the first week of August, it nearly always rains. Last year three guys turned up with a sauna as a raft. One of them was there purely to bail out water. That's a true story!

5000 people, 100 or so participants, upwards of 60 rafts. About 100 sponsors, 50 stallholders, and a rota of 25 street marshalls. This is the community resource that goes in to making the Raft Race happen every year. You come out soaking wet, but it's worth it. The Raft Race is one of those things that shouldn't work but does anyway.

If you're interested in participating, hit up the links below. See you in August!
